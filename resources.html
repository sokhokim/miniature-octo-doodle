Resources

Common therapy approaches explained simply.

This page organizes information already shared on the public website so visitors can understand the main models used in treatment.

EMDR

Eye-Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing is described as a structured, evidence-based treatment most commonly used for PTSD and addiction, involving traumatic memories and bilateral stimulation such as eye movements or tapping.

CBT

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is described as an evidence-based treatment for PTSD, anxiety, depression, and other concerns by helping clients identify, challenge, and reduce the impact of inaccurate or negative thinking.

Faith-integrated therapy

This approach creates space to process thoughts and feelings about faith, connect them with overall wellbeing, and draw from supports such as prayer, journaling, scripture-reading, or faith community for clients who want that perspective.
